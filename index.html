Who We Are

Building the Future of Space Robotics

Team Srishti is the official robotics team of Mar Athanasius College of Engineering, bringing together 20+ multidisciplinary students across all years of study.

We focus on designing autonomous systems for planetary exploration. Our current project is a state-of-the-art rover equipped with a robotic manipulator and autonomous drone, built for task execution on simulated Martian terrain.

Through the IRC, ERC, ARC and URC challenges, we push the boundaries of robotics and automation.

Current Rover

Prana

A fully autonomous planetary rover for Mars-simulated exploration. Prana has evolved through three major versions, each pushing the boundaries of autonomous robotics.

Version 1

First Prototype

The first prototype of Prana featured a 6-wheeled bogie equipped with a rocker-bogie suspension system — laying the foundational architecture for all subsequent iterations.

6-Wheel BogieRocker-Bogie Suspension
Version 2

Modular Overhaul

The second iteration created the current base — a modular four-wheeled bogie integrating independent swerve steering into an in-built Rocker Differential Kinematic Suspension system.

4-Wheel BogieSwerve SteeringKinematic Differential
Version 3 · Current

Latest Generation

The latest version features a refined kinematic differential suspension system, where the axes of the rocker arm and steering arm intersect at a single point — enabling superior terrain adaptability and stability.

Intersecting AxesRocker + Steering ArmAdvanced Kinematics

Where We Compete

Competitions

01

International Rover Challenge

Conducted by Space Robotic Society (SPROS)

A global space robotics engineering competition challenging university teams to design, develop and operate an astronaut-assistive next-generation planetary rover in Mars-simulated conditions.

02

European Rover Challenge

ERC · Poland

One of the world's largest space and robotics events, where teams globally compete with Mars rovers on a specially constructed Martian terrain replica.

03

University Rover Challenge

Mars Society · Utah, USA

The premier student robotics competition globally, held at the Mars Desert Research Station, testing rovers in real desert terrain.

04

Anatolian Rover Challenge

ARC · Turkey

An international planetary rover competition held in Turkey, testing teams on autonomous navigation, science tasks, and equipment servicing.
